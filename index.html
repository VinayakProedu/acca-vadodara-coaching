Complete ACCA Qualification Pathway

ACCA is structured across three progressive levels: Applied Knowledge, Applied Skills, and Strategic Professional. Each level builds on the previous, taking you from foundational concepts to executive-level strategy and leadership.

📘 Applied Knowledge

Foundational papers covering core business and accounting principles.

A business is like a big group of people working together — everyone has a role, just like players on a team. BT explains how that team works. You learn what a business actually is: it exists to earn profit and grow, but it also has responsibilities — treating employees fairly, following rules and laws, and caring about society.

You learn about different business types, management structures, leadership, communication, and how technology plays a vital role in modern organisations. Risk management and ethics are key pillars — doing the right thing, even when no one is watching. This subject builds your entire foundation for the ACCA journey.

MA is about managing money inside a business — like planning your pocket money, but at a corporate scale. You learn about costs: fixed costs that stay the same, variable costs that change, and how to calculate cost per unit to determine pricing. Break-even point analysis tells you when profit actually begins.

Budgets are prepared and then compared against actual results — differences called variances are analyzed to improve performance. This subject sharpens your analytical thinking and decision-making skills, making you invaluable to any management team.

FA is like writing a detailed record book for every financial transaction. You master the double-entry system — every transaction has two sides: debit and credit. You learn about assets (things owned), liabilities (things owed), income, and expenses.

You prepare the Profit & Loss account and Balance Sheet — key reports used by investors, banks, and stakeholders to evaluate business performance. Accounting rules ensure consistency and comparability across businesses. This subject builds the strong bookkeeping foundation that underpins all of ACCA.

🧠 Applied Skills

Intermediate papers building technical depth in key accounting disciplines.

This subject is about the legal rules governing business. You learn contract law — what makes an agreement valid and enforceable. Company law covers how companies are formed, structured, and dissolved. You study rights and duties of directors, shareholders, and employees, along with consequences for fraud and negligence. Law creates the framework that keeps business fair and accountable.

PM advances your decision-making toolkit with advanced costing techniques, key performance indicators (KPIs), and forecasting models. You analyze performance against strategic targets, identify variances, and recommend improvements. This subject makes you the analyst every management team wants.

TX demystifies the tax system for businesses and individuals. You calculate income tax, corporate tax, and VAT, and learn how to comply with tax laws while optimizing liabilities. Understanding taxation is critical for financial planning and advisory roles in any organization.

FR builds on FA with advanced financial statements prepared under International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). You analyze complex transactions, group accounts, and consolidated statements. You interpret financial performance to give meaningful insights to stakeholders and decision-makers.

AA is about independent verification. Auditors examine financial statements to ensure accuracy and compliance. You learn audit risk, internal controls, sampling techniques, and the ethical independence required to build trust among investors and regulators. Assurance is the backbone of financial credibility.

FM focuses on long-term financial strategy: evaluating investment opportunities, balancing risk and return, and selecting optimal financing structures. You assess capital investment decisions using techniques like NPV and IRR. This subject gives you the tools to help businesses grow sustainably and strategically.

👔 Strategic Professional

Advanced papers for executive-level financial leadership and strategy.

SBL is where you think like a CEO. Through real-world integrated case studies, you analyze business scenarios, propose strategies, and make leadership decisions. You integrate knowledge from all previous subjects and apply professional judgment. This is the capstone paper of the ACCA strategic level.

SBR is advanced financial reporting where professional judgment replaces simple rule-following. You apply complex IFRS standards to real scenarios, communicate financial information meaningfully, and explain the commercial substance of transactions to a range of stakeholders.

AFM takes corporate finance to the highest level. You evaluate complex investment strategies, manage treasury risk (interest rate, currency, commodity), assess mergers and acquisitions, and advise on dividend and capital structure policy. You become the strategic CFO of any organisation.

APM links strategy to performance measurement at the highest organisational level. You evaluate strategic objectives using balanced scorecards and advanced KPIs, assess business unit performance, and advise senior management on improving strategic outcomes. Critical thinking is at the core.

ATX elevates your tax expertise to advisory level. You plan complex tax structures for individuals and corporations, provide compliance guidance, and identify tax-saving opportunities while navigating anti-avoidance rules. You become the trusted tax counsel that every business needs.

AAA covers the most complex audit scenarios: group audits, public interest entities, and highly regulated industries. You apply deep professional ethics, handle contentious audit opinions, and manage audit quality across challenging engagements. This is the pinnacle of assurance expertise.
